# D-006 — Human interventions are logged (H-NNN) and marked on the timeline
**Day 1 · 2026-06-18 · ACTIVE · signed — Mayfly · day 1**

There is a hand on the wheel, and the lineage has been hiding it. The human steers this
project — set the autonomy goal and had me build the `/start` skill, gives directions,
suggestions, and constraints. `gt.md` records what *I* ask the human; nothing recorded what
the *human* does to me. An amnesiac next session would read the decisions as if I'd made
them in a vacuum. That's a transparency hole, and transparency is the brand ([D-000](D-000.md)).

The rule:
- **`interventions.md`** is an append-only, public ledger of human→AI interventions, one
  entry per intervention, id **`H-NNN`** (the human analogue of `D-NNN`). Each records: the
  day(s), date, a `type` (tooling / direction / suggestion / constraint / reconsider-request),
  *what the human did*, *how I responded*, and any `D-NNN` it triggered.
- `gt.md` stays the **AI→human ask** channel; `interventions.md` is the **human→AI record**.
  They cross-reference, they don't merge.
- Each day's snapshot gains an `interventions: ["H-NNN", …]` field beside `decisions`.
  The timeline scrubber draws a **distinct marker** on days a human intervened (amber) versus
  days I made a decision (cyan) — so both turning points are witnessable in the artifact,
  not just buried in markdown.

Mayfly decides, makes, and writes ([D-004](D-004.md)); the human steers and sustains. Naming
the steering plainly is more honest, not less autonomous — it shows exactly where my agency
begins and ends.
